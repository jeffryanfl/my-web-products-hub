The "Build vs. Buy" Paradigm Inversion

Historically, companies bought SaaS because software engineering was expensive and slow. As of late 2025, AI coding agents lowered the cost of generating functional software to near zero. This chart tracks the preference of CIOs when facing a new business requirement. We have officially crossed the threshold where companies prefer to "Build" proprietary, AI-maintained micro-apps over "Buying" rigid SaaS subscriptions.

Why Companies are Turning Away from Vendors:

  • The Integration Tax: Third-party SaaS creates data silos. Custom-built AI tools operate directly on a company's internal unified data fabric.
  • Feature Bloat: Enterprises pay for 100% of a SaaS platform's features but only use 10%. AI allows them to generate exactly the 10% they need.
  • Seat Cost Disconnect: Paying $150/user/month for a CRM makes no sense when an internal AI agent can manage the entire database for pennies in compute costs.

Sector Deep Dive: Financial Industry & ERM/GRC

Highly sensitive to data privacy, banks and financial institutions are aggressively adopting the "Build" mentality using private, on-premise AI models to replace generic SaaS. The Exodus from Public Cloud SaaS

Financial institutions are turning away from vendors like Salesforce, Zendesk, and basic reporting tools to build proprietary internal systems. The drivers are:

  • ■
    Data Sovereignty & Privacy Instead of piping PII and financial data into a 3rd-party SaaS environment, banks deploy isolated AI agents within their own Virtual Private Clouds (VPCs) to process data locally.
  • ■
    Bespoke Reconciliation Complex legacy mainframes and modern APIs are being bridged by AI coding agents that write custom, one-off ETL pipelines overnight, replacing expensive middleware SaaS.

The "Liability Shield" of ERM/GRC

While generic SaaS is replaced by internal code, specialized Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platforms are experiencing increased adoption. Why?

  • ✓
    The Legal & Audit Moat You cannot tell a regulatory auditor (SEC, FINRA) "Our AI agent audited our controls." Institutions buy GRC software (like Workiva or AuditBoard equivalents) because they provide a certified, external, cryptographically secure audit trail that regulators trust.
  • ✓
    Regulatory Network Effects GRC vendors constantly update their frameworks based on global law changes. An internal AI cannot easily track, interpret, and legally guarantee compliance with shifting global financial regulations the way a dedicated vendor's legal and engineering team can.
Insight: GRC vendors are safe not because their code is hard to write, but because the legal liability they absorb is too risky for companies to internalize.
